Life Sciences Institute

UX Research project to resolve contact management problems



Methods

Contextual Inquiry & Affinity mapping


Duration

12 weeks

Role

UX Researcher


Team

Chuhan Hou, Mandy Shen, & Neha Dhawan

Problem Statement

Reduce manual effort to collect, maintain, manage, update, and cross check contact information from various sources.

The Communications team has to reach out to variety of stakeholders of the institute to spread the word about the institute. Hence, they require updated contact information of these stakeholders periodically. The stakeholders include faculty, postdoctoral students, graduate student researchers, donors, alumni, centres, and board members, however their contact information t is not located in one centralized system but is fragmented and stored on different systems in different formats.

Each team at the institute uses different tools to fulfill their requirements and there is no single centralized data management. Following image displays the tools from which the Communications team collects and manages their data:


During the interview phase, we followed the contextual inquiry method and collected our qualitative data through interpreting the interview recordings into individual statements. Strictly following the method of Contextual inquiry, we conducted all the seven interviews at interviewees’ work space, so we can look above and beyond the proposed problem. All the interviewees were administrative staff within LSI belonging to Communications, Human Resource, Development, Academic Program, and Operations team. After all the interviews, we did the interpretation sessions and came up with an affinity wall to analyze the qualitative data we collected through interviews.

Key Findings:

  • The Communications team spends substantial manual effort to cross check contact information in MCommunity and update it in Insightly.
  • The alumni information is outdated because the alumnis belong to different schools and there is no consistent system to keep track of their information.
  • The lack of assignment of titles to non-institute-appointed people and the existence of multiple titles and affiliations are causing problems for the Communications team.
  • The Communications team has to manually standardize the contact information generated from DART to make it compatible with the tools they use.
  • It is hard to manually enter information from business cards obtained through various networking events.

The ideal solution is to have one centralized data management system. Although this ideal solution is not feasible, it does serve as a general guideline for us to brainstorm for recommendations.

Key Recommendations:

  • Integrating MCommunity People API to automate the cross checking process of contact information data in Insightly.
  • Request personal individual contact information during onboarding process and cross check it during offboarding to build the future alumni database.
  • Standardize job role categorizations and meanings for a smoother communication process.
  • Leverage the Excel built-in feature Macro to help with data-massaging contact information from DART.
  • Use business card reader to quickly populate data from business card and expand contacts’ profiles through online-searching features of these products.
