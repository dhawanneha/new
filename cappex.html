Cappex

Redesigning a website to help high school students search for colleges

Methods

User Interviews, Comparative Analysis, Heuristic Evaluation, Surveys, Persona, Sketching, Paper Prototyping, & Low Fidelity Prototype

Duration

12 weeks

Role

UX Researcher & Designer


Team

Crystal Shan, Juliana Lam, Neha Dhawan, Yilin Xue, & Yuan Lee


Problem Statement

Cappex experiences lower rate of engagement as compared to its competitors and wants to evaluate its search functionality.

Motivation

We started with the heuristic evaluation of the current website to figure out the problems in the current flow. Followed by that we conducted the competitive analysis with four websites such as Big Future (College Board), Niche, US News, and Chegg. We also conducted analysis of good search engines such as Google and Amazon. In addition, we looked into the filter features of Amazon and Airbnb to give us a decent idea of the industry wide practices.

We then designed an interview protocol and contacted 16 high school students for interviews. After getting results from the interview, we now became aware of the unknowns and could include it in our survey logic. We sent out our survey to the freshman students at the University of Michigan. We got 60 survey results.

With results from interviews and surveys, we validated our 5 preliminary personas we had created through our logic and revised them as required.

Major Findings From Interviews And Surveys

Top things that students value:

  • Location
  • Finance
  • Job prospects
  • Major
  • Reputation
  • Feel of the college
  • They are not sure what they should look for.

Paper Prototypes

All team members then came up with wireframes based on our research. The five sets of wireframes was combined into two main themes and we took feedback on both of these.

  • One was after the user logins and the search results are displayed based on the profile and data entered by the user. This makes the search more customized and might be more relevant.
  • The second method gave the users the flexibility to only login if they want to and they could filter their search results based on their preferences.

After getting feedback, we decided to focus on the second theme where user can explore the website without logging in.
