ZipTrip

Designing a real time carpooling app for students at University of Michigan



Methods

User Interviews, Persona, Crazy Eight, Storyboarding, Journey Mapping, Sketching, Paper Prototyping, & Low Fidelity Prototype

Duration

12 weeks

Role

UX Designer



Project

Individual


Problem Statement

Students at the University of Michigan who live off campus don't have affordable and frequent commute solutions

Overview

This individual project stemmed from my own experiences of inefficient and inconvenient commute to college campus or grocery shopping especially on weekends.

I observed other students waiting up to half an hour for the bus after purchasing groceries. Most of them generally have a commute of 15 minutes or less but they usually cannot walk back comfortably with their groceries. The bus rides are free but they are infrequent on weekends and during the late hours which makes commute inconvenient.

Validation of problem

I conducted seven interviews with students. I asked them about commuting experiences. The major themes that recurred in these interviews were grocery shopping. So, I asked them about their grocery habits, process for grocery shopping, time taken to do groceries, and means of transportation. The problem validation phase made me realize that commute on the whole could be improved.





Target Audience

Target Audience The primary audience of my project are students without a car who require better solution to do groceries or generally commute in an affordable manner.

Competitive Analysis

I considered The Ride (bus service), Uber, Zipcar, InstaCart (grocery delivery app), ArborBike (Bike Sharing program), and IShareaRide (Carpooling for work) for competitive analysis.

Research findings

Ann Arbor is a college town where two types of car owners might be interested in carpooling:

  • The students who would like to have company and also make some extra money by carpooling.
  • Students and working population who own car want to carpool to meet new people, help students, and reduce carbon print.

Census Bureau, 2016

  • More than 40% households in Ann Arbor have 2 cars and more than 33% of the household have 1 car
  • Driving alone is the most popular method of commute and is adopted by 55% population



Design Rationale

I used Questions, Options, and Criteria to come up with the features of the app. It made me think critically about each way to solve a problem via different features to evaluate and compare all my options.

Description Of How Car Owner And Riders Are Matched

  • Location based matching: The current location and destination is considered to match both parties. The rider and car owner are matched if they are located within 0.5 miles of each other, and they are going in the same direction.
  • Number of seats: The car owners list the number of seats on offer and the riders specify the number of seats required.
  • Bid for a ride: The riders and car owners bid for a particular ride. Both parties can view the average price of the trip listed by the other party.
  • Pick up point and drop off point: The app generates pick up points and drop off points that are convenient for both parties; located within 0.25 miles of the rider which takes 5 minutes of walk.

Societal Impact

ZipTrip might help in saving time and money for students. It would provide an affordable option to the students besides the Ride to commute. If car owners are students, they might make some pocket money by carpooling and meet other new students. Other residents and working professionals might have sense of fulfilment by giving rides to people who need it and help in saving energy and reducing carbon footprint. Also, since Ann Arbor has huge international student population, this app will promote conversations among people from different cultural and national backgrounds.
